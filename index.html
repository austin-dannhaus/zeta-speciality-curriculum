Our Philosophy

Framework First,
Then Exemplars

Zeta's specialist teachers are already experts in their crafts. The challenge isn't teaching them how to teach chess or dance — it's giving them a coherent, consistent framework so students across every school build skills at the same pace and to the same standard.

We approach this work by establishing unit-level benchmarks and assessment tools first, then building exemplar lesson plans that demonstrate the standard. Teachers get a clear target and a model path — not a script. This preserves autonomy, scales across a diverse teacher pool, and ensures programmatic consistency network-wide.

We also believe this project is too large and too important to get right in one pass. We'll begin with a scoped pilot (Unit 1 + two content areas) to stress-test templates and refine the framework before going wide. The result of the pilot informs every subsequent unit delivery.

🎯

Benchmark-Led Design

Every unit begins with measurable outcomes by grade band. Assessment tools are designed before lesson plans — so teachers always know what "mastery" looks like.

🏗️

Templates as Infrastructure

A single master template set governs all six subjects, all three program types, and all grade bands. Changes to format propagate universally — no inconsistency across content areas.

🔄

Pilot Before Scale

Unit 1 across two subjects serves as the proof-of-concept. Jen reviews and approves the template and quality standard before the full production run begins.

📐

Differentiation Built In

Every deliverable reflects all four grade bands (K–2, 3–5, 6–8, 9+) and all three program models (Classes/Electives, Z Clubs, Z Teams). No retrofitting after the fact.

🎓

Academic Rigor for Specialty

Each content area will carry the same academic weight as core subjects — content vocabulary, cultural history, skill progression, and trackable data — making the case network-wide.

📅

Rolling Release Schedule

Units are delivered 6–8 weeks before implementation begins, giving teachers and school leaders time to review, prepare materials, and attend training.

How to Read These Ranges

Each range reflects the same scope delivered at two levels of lesson plan depth. The primary lever is number of exemplar lesson plans per grade band per subject — 2 plans puts you at the low end; 3 plans with full program differentiation shown puts you at the high end. Final depth is confirmed with Jen Loving after the Phase 0 pilot review, at which point Phases 2–5 can be re-estimated with much tighter confidence.

  • Milestone payments — each phase invoiced upon approved delivery, not on a retainer schedule.
  • One revision round per phase is included. Additional rounds billed at role rates against the contingency reserve.
  • Phase 0 is the entry commitment — scope and pricing for Phases 1–5 can be formally confirmed after the pilot review in mid-June.
